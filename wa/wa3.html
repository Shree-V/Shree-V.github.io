What is Bharathanatyam?

Bharathanatyam is an Indian Classical dance form that originated in the South Indian state of Tamilnadu. It is a combination of Bhava, Raga, Thala, and Natyam otherwise known as emotion, melody, rhythm, and dance hence the name Bharathanatyam.It is one out of eight of the main forms of Classical dance recognized in India.

History

The first theoretical foundations of Bharathanatyam are found in the Natya Shastra which is an ancient Sanskrit text by Bharatha Muni that is dated between 200 BCE and 200 CE. It details everything from different dance pieces, to the theory of expression and emotions, to postures and gestures, to basic steps, to acting techniques, etc. Historically, Bharathanatyam has been all about bringing spiritual and religious stories and teachings to life through dance. The art form was banned during the British rule of India. But, it was revived by various artists during the Indian Independence Movement in the early 20th century.

Bharathanatyam Today

Since the revival of Bharathanatyam in the early 20th century, it has evolved into a more modern art form. It has become more modern in two different senses. On one hand, it is now used to tell a wider variety of stories instead of just stories from Indian mythology and Hinduism. Now, Bharathanatyam is used to tell not only spiritual stories but also stories that resonate with a much broader audience. On another hand, the movements and the way that Bharathanatyam is performed has become much more theatrical and intricate to maintain uniqueness in each piece and engage the audience for longer periods of time. In short, Bharathanatyam has a much bigger focus on the audience now than it did before.
